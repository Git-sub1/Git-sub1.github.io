Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

For this part, I repeated the same procedure from Part 1 but in an urban setting instead of a selfie. The zoomed-in photo flattens the scene, making buildings and objects look closer together and compressed. The wide-angle photo from up close exaggerates depth, stretching the scene so distances feel larger. This difference comes from perspective: zoom narrows the field of view and reduces depth cues, while wide angle expands the field and emphasizes depth.
